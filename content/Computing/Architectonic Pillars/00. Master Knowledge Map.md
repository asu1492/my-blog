## В§ 1 вҖ” Philosophy: Why This Vault Exists

Computer Science, at its core, is the discipline of managing complexity. Every problem an engineer faces maps to one of three fundamental concerns:

1. **Organising Data** вҖ” how information is structured, searched, and manipulated efficiently (DSA, algorithms, complexity)
2. **System** вҖ” how complexity is distributed and managed across machines and time (architecture, infrastructure, databases)
3. **Programming Paradigm & Framework** вҖ” how human intention is translated into executable form (languages, patterns, frameworks)

These three are not independent. They are three lenses on the same problem. A senior engineer switches between them fluidly: choosing the right data structure (Organising Data) to implement a service (Programming) that will scale (System).

The **fourth pillar вҖ” Emergent Tech** вҖ” is not a separate concern but an integrative one. Agents, LLMs, and MCP-based systems *synthesise* the other three: they use algorithms to reason (Organising Data), expose and consume APIs (System), and are built with SDKs and frameworks (Programming).
